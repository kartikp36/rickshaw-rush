# How to Publish Rickshaw Rush 3D to the Google Play Store

This guide contains everything you need to know to take the Android version of your game and put it on the Google Play Store.

The Android app was generated using a tool called **Capacitor**, which wraps your existing HTML/JS code inside a native Android container.

## Step 1: Install Android Studio

1. Download **Android Studio** from the official site: https://developer.android.com/studio
2. Install it on your computer following the standard installation steps for your operating system (Windows, macOS, or Linux).
3. During the first launch, follow the setup wizard to ensure the **Android SDK** and **Android SDK Command-line Tools** are installed.

## Step 2: Prepare your Code

Before opening the game in Android Studio, you must compile your game files and sync them into the Android project. Open a terminal or command prompt in this project's folder and run:

1. `npm install` (to install the required build tools)
2. `npm run build` (to copy your game files into a web directory)
3. `npx cap sync` (to sync those files into the Android project)

## Step 3: Open your Game in Android Studio

1. Open Android Studio.
2. Click on **Open**.
3. Navigate to the folder where you have downloaded/cloned this repository on your computer.
4. Select the `android` folder (which was generated by Capacitor) and click **Open**.
5. *Note: Android Studio will begin a process called "Gradle Sync". This can take a few minutes. Wait for it to complete successfully before moving on.*

## Step 4: Test the App on an Emulator or Real Device

1. Once Gradle Sync is complete, you can test the game!
2. At the top of Android Studio, you'll see a play button (▶).
3. Select an emulator from the dropdown (or plug in your Android phone via USB and enable USB Debugging) and click the play button to launch the game.

## Step 5: Register a Google Play Developer Account

If you haven't already:
1. Go to the [Google Play Console](https://play.google.com/console).
2. Sign in with your Google account.
3. Follow the steps to register as a developer. This requires a one-time fee of $25.
4. Verify your identity as prompted.

## Step 6: Build the App for Release

To upload to the Play Store, you need an **Android App Bundle (.aab)** file.

1. In Android Studio, go to the top menu and select **Build > Generate Signed Bundle / APK...**
2. Choose **Android App Bundle** and click **Next**.
3. Under "Key store path", click **Create new...**
   - Choose a safe location on your computer to save this keystore file (do NOT lose this file or the passwords, you need it for all future updates to your game).
   - Fill out the passwords and the "Certificate" details (your name/company name).
   - Click **OK**.
4. Make sure your newly created keystore is selected, enter the passwords, and click **Next**.
5. Check the **release** box and click **Finish**.
6. Wait for Android Studio to build the release bundle. It will show a popup when it's done, with a link to locate the file (usually inside `android/app/release/app-release.aab`).

## Step 7: Create the App in the Play Console

1. Go to your [Google Play Console](https://play.google.com/console).
2. Click **Create app**.
3. Fill in the App name ("Rickshaw Rush 3D"), default language, and select "Game".
4. Choose whether it's Free or Paid (Free is standard for ad-supported games).
5. Accept the Developer Program Policies and click **Create app**.

## Step 8: Fill out Store Details and Upload

The Play Console dashboard will guide you through the required steps. You will need to:

1. **Set up your app:** Answer questions about your app's content, target audience, privacy policy, and whether it uses ads (select "No" for now, since we will add ads in the next phase).
2. **Main Store Listing:** Upload screenshots, a high-res icon (512x512), a feature graphic (1024x500), and write a short and long description.
3. **Upload the AAB:**
   - Go to **Production** on the left menu.
   - Click **Create new release**.
   - Upload the `.aab` file you generated in Android Studio.
   - Enter release notes (e.g., "Initial release!").
   - Click **Review release** and then **Rollout to Production**.

Your app will go into a review phase by Google (this can take a few days). Once approved, it will be live on the Play Store!
